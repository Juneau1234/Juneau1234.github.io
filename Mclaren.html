Learn more about Mclaren

McLaren was founded in 1963 by New Zealand racing driver Bruce McLaren, originally as a motorsport team before expanding into road cars. The brand is best known for its cutting-edge engineering and Formula 1 dominance, but when it comes to road cars, the McLaren F1 is often considered its most legendary model. Launched in the 1990s, the F1 held the record for the world’s fastest production car for years. The most expensive McLaren ever sold is the McLaren F1 LM-Specification, which went for $19.8 million at auction. McLaren continues to push boundaries with hypercars like the P1 and the Speedtail. Unlike Ferrari, McLaren doesn’t restrict who can buy their cars, but their top-tier models still remain ultra-exclusive due to their high price tags and limited production numbers.
